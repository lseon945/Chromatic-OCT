# Chromatic optical coherence tomography
Optical coherence tomography (OCT) stands as a critical tool in medical imaging, providing essential insights into tissue microstructures. In particular, high-resolution OCT, with resolutions ranging from 1 to 3 µm, offers significant potential for subcellular diagnostics compared to the 10 to 20 µm resolution of conventional OCT. However, the use of high-numerical-aperture objective lenses necessary for such high resolutions inevitably reduces the depth of focus (DOF), limiting practical applications of OCT. Here, we present a novel chromatic OCT system that achieves high resolution and high signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) over an extended DOF. Our system achieves an isotropic high spatial resolution of 2 to 3 µm and extends the DOF by up to eight-fold by using sample arm optics with a substantial chromatic focal shift and a high numerical aperture, along with a broad bandwidth light source. Additionally, we developed an algorithm for enhancing the SNR and for artifact removal. Both theoretical simulations and experimental assessments confirm that our chromatic OCT system surpasses conventional high-resolution OCT in maintaining enhanced resolution and SNR over a wider imaging range. This advancement greatly enhances the potential for high-resolution deep tissue imaging in biomedical applications, significantly improving diagnostic capabilities.
